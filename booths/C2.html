The Chinese Guqin, also known as the Yaoqin or the Seven-string Zither, is one of China's most ancient plucked instruments, boasting a history of over three thousand years.

Holding the foremost position among the Four Arts (Guqin, Go/Weiqi, Calligraphy, and Painting), the Guqin has been a compulsory art of self-cultivation for Chinese gentlemen since antiquity. The classic tale of Bo Ya and Zi Qi, who achieved soulmate status through the melody of High Mountains and Flowing Water, has been passed down through the ages. The zither's image permeates Chinese literature, poetry, calligraphy, and painting, embodying the profound cultural significance of spiritual nourishment and moral refinement.

We sincerely invite all guests to pause for a moment of rest. Relax your mind to the gentle melodies of the Guqin, enjoy the beautiful afternoon.
